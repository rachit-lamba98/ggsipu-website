Objective

When knowledge and learning are associated solely with economics and development and ‘are amputated from ethics, then an overarching conception of human good and violence against humanity becomes banal. Moral choices are not always black and white, but they still have to be made.’ The Education System is at a crossroad in our country.

In the initial years of its growth, the University’s prime focus was on teaching of the professional courses, with a clientele of Delhi based students in majority. Now after more than 15 years of its presence in Delhi as an education hub, we need to gear up for the new challenges such as privatisation of Higher Education, declining morale of the academicians, leading to declining teaching commitments, improving the quality of the scholars and their research output, balancing teaching and research commitments. Individual disciplines have their own distinct requirements and we cannot have narrowly laid down specifications for each.

Besides, Universities should be the spaces where free dialogue and exchange of ideas takes place. The purpose of a university cannot be limited to providing vocational education to its students. In order to make the students and faculty of the university engage with ideas of higher interests, it is pertinent that they should be given orientation and training for critical and independent thinking.

Our University asserts its identity as a professional university, but the question which comes to fore is whether professional university means a disconnect from the society and national and international concerns? Does this mean that traditional disciplines have no place in professional programmes?

In the light of this, it is imperative to hold open-ended discussions, with a view to prepare a blueprint for higher education in our University in particular and for the higher education as a national policy in general.  

Regular seminars, debates, Colloquium, symposia, workshops are essential on such matters for we, in the University, are shaping one invaluable human resource, the youth of India, tomorrow’s future for the Nation and the world. Instilling a sense of professionalism with deep commitments to the cause of society and humanity has to be ingrained side by side. A socially sensitive individual with a strong sense of purpose and self-discipline is an essential pre-requisite for a nation constantly trying to strengthen its democratic traditions against the patriarchal, feudal, irrational and regressive mindsets.
